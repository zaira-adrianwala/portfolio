I design and test hardware for sensing and stimulation in biomedical systems.

I’m a bioengineering M.Eng student at UC Berkeley with experience in circuit design, embedded systems, bioinstrumentation, and PCB strategy.

I like working on problems where I can connect physiology with electronics: building circuits that sense what the body is doing, creating stimulation hardware, and making sure everything is testable and reliable in the lab. My recent work includes a CPR coaching device, a brain-on-chip platform, and hardware to pace and stretch cardiac tissue.
